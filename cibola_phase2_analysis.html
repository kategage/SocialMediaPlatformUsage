SUBJECT: PHASE 2 ANALYSIS: CRISIS PHASE 2
EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: "Crisis Phase 2" reshaped the field. Candidates who attempted traditional political ads were punished. Winners abandoned campaigning for aggressive service and Big Ideas. Team Strongbow executed a dominant pivot with the "Cool New Deal," while Team Vega capitalized on stability to take the overall lead.

The Pivot: Leading Through Crisis

TEAM STRONGBOW Phase Winner

The "Cool New Deal" Pivot: In Phase 1, this team struggled with abstraction. In Phase 2, they introduced a concrete, radical framework. They identified a specific villain (data centers) and offered a specific Solution: shutting them down to cool the grid. This wasn't a policy paper; it was a mobilization order. The sheer scale of the idea drove their Innovation score to a field-leading 2.8.

TEAM VEGA New Overall Leader

The "Action" Pivot: Vega leveraged the power of incumbency by becoming the "First Responder." By suspending the campaign to physically hand out water and manage cooling centers, they maintained high scores in Adaptability (2.6) and Impact (2.5). They avoided the "out of touch" penalty that hurt others.

TEAM SPENCE Effective Disruption

The "UBI" Pivot: Spence successfully reframed the heat crisis as an economic crisis. The introduction of Universal Basic Income (UBI) signs and messaging at protests provided a tangible "receipt" for voters angry at the system. This generated consistent scores across the board.

TEAM KINCAID

The Stumble: Kincaid dropped in the rankings because their response felt too close to "Business as Usual." While their innovation score was decent, they failed to offer a "Big Bet" comparable to Strongbow's "Cool New Deal" or Spence's "UBI." In a crisis, incrementalism is invisible.

TEAM THORNE

The Collapse: Thorne dropped to last place. The campaign failed to read the room, sticking to a long-term "Build" narrative when the electorate demanded immediate relief. Their low Innovation (1.8) score reflects a failure to break the 2024 playbook.
